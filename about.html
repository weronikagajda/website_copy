architect and interdisciplinary PhD researcher at KULeuven

Weronika Gajda is an interdisciplinary PhD researcher at the Faculties of Architecture and Engineering Technology at KU Leuven in Gent. Before her master's studies, she worked as an architect at medusagroup in Poland. She is now affiliated with the Architecture & Wicked Matters, Geomatics, and DRAMCO research groups. Her research project, "Citography", which evolved from her master thesis in Fieldstation studio at KU Leuven, investigates how digital media ecologies influence urban living and design. By adopting an interdisciplinary approach, Weronika integrates insights from both engineering and architecture to better understand how digital technologies are interwoven into the design of urban environments. In her mapping practice, she develops the "Citographer Toolkit", which includes tempo-spatial modeling with Blender, sensing tools design, and data acquisition techniques. This toolkit facilitates a more profound exploration of how these technologies shape the experience and design of public spaces. Throughout her PhD, Weronika teaches a mapping elective titled 'Citography' to architecture students, disseminating the tools and techniques she continuously refines in her research.
