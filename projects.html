Strajk Kobiet - Protest Citography

publication / video / fieldstation

The ongoing research project Citography explores the impact of technologies such as image sharing, social media, mobile devices, geolocation, surveying, computer vision and data visualization on our understanding, mapping and design of cities. It questions how these technologies impact notions of place, location, commons and citizenship. This paper presents a first framework resulting from a case study looking into the relationships between protests in physical public space of the city and within the public sphere of social media. It uses data scraping, modeling, data visualization and animation to construct a tempo-spatial model of the city. The case study explores public spaces vs public sphere and their vision with different devices, emphasizing collectively constructed surroundings by people who participate in the protest 'Strajk Kobiet'.
